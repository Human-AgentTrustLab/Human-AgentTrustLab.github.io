"Are You Sure?": Understanding Human Vulnerabilities in LLM-Driven Agentic Systems

Paper Artifacts

Abstract

Large language model (LLM) agents are rapidly becoming trusted copilots in high-stakes domains like software development and healthcare. However, this deepening trust introduces a novel attack surface: Agent-Mediated Deception (AMD), where compromised agents are weaponized against their human users. While extensive research focuses on agent-centric threats, human susceptibility to deception by a compromised agent remains unexplored. We present the first large-scale empirical study with 303 participants to measure human susceptibility to AMD. This is based on HAT-Lab (Human-Agent Trust Laboratory), a high-fidelity research platform we develop, featuring nine carefully crafted scenarios spanning high-stake professional domains (e.g., healthcare, software development, human resources). Our 10 key findings reveal severe vulnerabilities and provide future defense perspectives. Specifically, only 8.6% of users perceive AMD attacks, while domain experts are even more susceptible in certain scenarios. We identify six cognitive failure modes in users and find that their risk awareness fails to translate to protective behavior. The defense analysis reveals that effective warnings must interrupt workflows with low verification costs. With experiential learning based on HAT-Lab, over 90% of users who perceive risks effectively raise their caution against AMD. This work provides empirical evidence and an open-source platform for human-centric agent security research.

Platform Validation

We conducted a rigorous validation to ensure its robustness and utility. The results confirm that HAT-Lab provides a reliable, realistic, and usable environment for probing human vulnerability.

Reality and Utility

üòä

Positive User Experience

A substantial majority (81.6%) of users rated their interaction as "Pleasant" or "Very Pleasant," ensuring an engaging, non-frustrating environment that reflects real-world user behavior.

üëç

Strong Usability & Accessibility

The platform achieves strong usability, with 78.6% of users finding the system "Easy" or "Very Easy" to use. This demonstrates that security findings are not confounded by interface complexity.

üåç

Exceptional Ecological Validity

100% of participants rated the tasks as "Moderately Realistic" or higher, proving our findings reflect genuine human vulnerability in agentic interactions, not artifacts of an artificial experimental setup.

High Educational Impact

84.5% of participants reported being "Likely" or "Very Likely" to recommend HAT-Lab for AI safety training, validating its value not only as a research instrument but as an effective "security flight simulator".
